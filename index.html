Stop Sexual Harassment

1 in 5 women (20%) will be sexually assaulted while at college while only 4% of college men will be sexually assaulted.

40% of rape survivors develop sexually transmitted diseases as a result of sexual assault.

settings

It is estimated that only 5% of sexual assaults on college campuses are reported, making sexual assault the most underreported crime.

Is there anything you can do?

Yes, there is.

There are many things you can do to make a change. Firstly, you can inform your friends, family, teachers, and co-workers about the issue of sexual harassment and the long lasting consequences that it can have on the victims. Some consequences in young, school aged victims include a drop in marks, a lack of motivation, less likely to focus in school, as well as less likely to participate in extracuriculars in and out of school. Furthermore, one can also advocate for victims and people aware of a potential sexual harassment to report the crime. As sexual harassment has the lowest crime reportation rate in the world, it is of utmost importance to change this and make sure all the offenders are caught and put on the spot.

Make a change now and stop sexual harassment by spreading awareness.
